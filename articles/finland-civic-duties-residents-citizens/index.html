Civic duties of residents and citizens in Finland

Living in Finland involves not only rights and freedoms, but also civic duties. These duties help ensure that society functions fairly, safely, and effectively, and that public institutions can serve the population as a whole.

Obligation to obey the law

All residents and citizens in Finland are required to obey the law. Laws apply equally to everyone and form the foundation of a society governed by the rule of law. This obligation is central to legal certainty and public trust.

In practice, obeying the law means respecting decisions made by courts, following regulations issued by authorities, and acting within the limits set by legislation. It also means accepting legal consequences if the law is violated.

Respect for the law protects the rights of others and ensures that conflicts are resolved through lawful and peaceful means.

Taxation and public contributions

Paying taxes is one of the most important civic duties in Finland. Taxes finance public services such as education, healthcare, social security, infrastructure, and public safety. These services benefit society as a whole.

Tax obligations are based on income and personal circumstances, and the tax system is designed to be progressive. This means that those with higher incomes generally contribute a larger share.

By paying taxes correctly and on time, residents and citizens help maintain the Finnish welfare state and ensure equal access to essential services.

Participation in society

Civic duties also include contributing to society through work, study, and community involvement. Active participation supports economic stability and social cohesion.

Participation may take many forms, such as working, studying, volunteering, or engaging in local community activities. These contributions strengthen mutual trust and shared responsibility.

While participation is often voluntary, it reflects the expectation that individuals take part in building and maintaining a functioning society.

Special duties of citizens

Some civic duties apply specifically to Finnish citizens. These duties are linked to full membership in the state and the rights that citizenship provides.

Such duties may include obligations related to national defence and participation in certain civic responsibilities when required by law. These duties exist to protect society and ensure its continued functioning.

Fulfilling these responsibilities reflects the balance between rights and duties that characterises citizenship in Finland.

Why civic duties matter

Civic duties balance individual rights and freedoms. They promote fairness, shared responsibility, and trust between individuals and institutions. Without civic duties, the rights guaranteed by law could not function effectively in practice.
